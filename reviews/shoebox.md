## Niko B – Shoebox
## Track Review

Before we dive too deep into this, I have to warn you — this is a slightly different review for me. This time I’ll be focusing primarily on the producer behind the track, Killthissonny.

Let me explain myself. I love Niko B. I think he blends personality, comedy and wordplay into a genre that can sometimes take itself a little too seriously. And he does all this while still putting out proper bangers like Tresspass Coat, It’s Not Litter If You Bin It, Love Island Freestyle and Sell It!

This time though, we’re discussing a track from his latest album cheerleader — “Shoebox”.

When I tell you this beat is actually crack, I’m not exaggerating. This might genuinely be one of the most infectious, catchy and ear-pleasing beats I’ve heard in a long time. It’s the kind of instrumental that makes you annoyed you didn’t think of it first. Seriously — this is one of those beats you could pass down for generations. It’s that cold.

From beat one, we’re met with a synth that persists throughout the track. It sounds like it’s driven by a punchy LFO, maybe landing on a 1/4-time pulse. Beyond that, you can just hear how rich the chord is. It pulls you in instantly and doesn’t let go.

But what really locks you in is the drop. The chords run until around 00:12, then suddenly everything cuts for two seconds. When it comes back, we’re hit with this nasally yet punchy bass, bouncy trap drums, and that same addictive synth tying it all together. There’s something about it that’s hard to even explain — you just have to hear it.

And there’s one more detail I have to mention: what I’m pretty sure is a snare roll, first noticeable around 0:22. It’s subtle, but it runs through the full drum loop, and it makes such a difference. It’s one of those small production choices that completely elevates a track. It might sound minor on paper, but once you hear it, you’ll get it.

Now… onto my one criticism.

The reason I’ve focused so heavily on the production is because, on this track, the producer slightly outshines Niko. That’s not to say Niko can’t rap — he absolutely can, and there are great performances on this album. But here, I find myself wishing for a bit more.

In a way, the beat almost feels wasted — not because Niko doesn’t have the talent to do it justice, but because it doesn’t feel perfectly suited to his strengths. I genuinely think this is one of the best UK beats in recent memory, despite how simple it is, and I can’t help but imagine how crazy it might sound with an artist whose style leans more naturally into this pocket.

Again, this isn’t a knock on Niko. If anything, it’s praise for Killthissonny. This review is less about criticising Niko and more about highlighting one of the most promising producers working right now.

If you love this track and want more from Sonny, check out Sell It!, Conglomerate (he produced a big portion of it), and Sirens (From Ireland). He’s also collaborated extensively with artists like Lancey Foux, Fimi, and even JELEEL! — which says a lot about his range.

## Final Verdict

Niko definitely gives a solid performance here. But if we’re being honest, this is a track people will keep coming back to for the beat. I can’t even lie — it’s that good.

A slightly lighter review this time. Thought I’d ease the vibe a bit after the roasting I gave Mink a few weeks ago.

RATING: 8.3/10
