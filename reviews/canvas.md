## Jdrly - Canvas

## TRACK REVIEW

jdrly is an artist that's exploded in popularity in the past few months, largely in part to his track "canvas". He's been heavily promoting it on tiktok (which is where I found it), he's been mad consistent on the promotion though, I think a lot of newer artists could definitley learn a lot from his approach. The track samples aphex twin's Xtal, a genius flip in my opinion, aphex is like a cheatcode to cloud rap that not enough people exploit.

To breakdown the track for you, its the dreamy aphex flip, a bouncy trap beat, and JD's vocals. As you can see the composition is very simple, but it's how these 3 parts work together, along with the attention to detail that result in an incredibly infectious take on uk cloud rap. To disect each part, we'll start with the sample flip... I am a massive fan of sampling, in my opinion it is the backbone of all genres of hiphop, and one of the hardest things to master. The aphex sample flip is the backbone of this track, it's such an incredibly fitting sample for a cloud rap track, the sought that makes you as a producer kick yourself for not thinking of first. It's so bouncy and catchy that whemn you hear it for the first time, you just wanrt to put it on repeat.

Moving om from the sample, the drum beat behind this track compliments the intended sound so well, the way it kicks in on the drop is so pleasing, and it's just so consistently good, the way it cuts out on. some bars, then comes back in to create another moment. 

What matters most to me though, and this is more of a personal thing, is that JD is doing all of this from his bedroom. He's making the beat, performing the lyrics, and promoting the track all from his room. This is exactly why I love the UK scene so much, it's young people in their rooms who got an SM57 and Logic pro for their birthday producing, mixing and performing music from their bedroom that you've never heard before.

Of course I could find criticisms in this track, it's by no means perfect for sure, but I wanted this review to focus more on the context behind the music, rather than pure analytical breakdown. I feel that JD is a great example of why the UK scene is thriving so much right now, it's pure love for music and passion to create. UK rap has always been like this though, we had the guys grinding out CD's and mixtapes like Dizzee Rascal and Chip, then we had the legendary Youtube freestyles from BBK, and now we have a crazy mix of a thriving mainstream scene, and equally as strong underground scene. In my opinion, Uk rap is as strong as it's ever been, and I can't wait to see what's next

In conclusion, Canvas, while relatively simple, is an incredibly creative and catchy track that deservedly has propelled JD's career massively, and since the drop of "White Nose" (Which has also gone crazy), I think he knows his Niche and path to success is through cloud rap. 

## Track Rating: 7.0/10
