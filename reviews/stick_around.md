## Saam Sultan - Stick Around

## TRACK REVIEW

Saam Sultan is an artist making crazy buzz, pushing past the label of “UK underground” and more towards a mainstream force, and I think that his success is entirely deserved. To prove this, I present to you “Stick Around”. This track melds crazy synths, airy vocals and spacey drums to present a track that envelopes you in its vibe; the delay on the vocals, the beat drops — everything about this track is an experience curated by Saam.

The track begins with an introduction to these incredible synths. That first synth chord hits so hard, instantly giving you a taste of what you’re about to experience. We are then met with Saam’s vocals. While his lyrics may be relatively simple — take “They bite my flows, velociraptor” for example — he makes up for this in his ability to cater his vocals to the track. A song like this does not need complex lyrics with deep meaning; it is intended to immerse rather than be dissected. This song is pure ear candy. Lyrical depth almost feels irrelevant. We’re here for one thing: the vibe.

The suspense built from the intro culminates in this transcending beat drop at 00:43, where Saam introduces it through the namesake of the song, “Stick Around”, where he adds this incredible delay to his vocals that complements this dreamy sound so well. For me, Saam’s vocals are merely an instrument in this song, used to accompany and complement the synths. He utilises his voice as an addition to the track, not the definition.

I really can’t overstate how incredible the synths are in this song; the way they both build and satisfy the suspense of the track is incredible. They are so layered, so pleasing to the ear, and sound as if they were made for the sole purpose of this song.

The attention to detail in this track is also a testament to Saam’s artistry: the subtle guitar in the chorus, the intricate, deep mixing on the vocals, the stuttering, the reverb — all of it really shows how in tune Saam is with his sound, like he knows exactly what is needed to perfect a sound.

For as good as this track is, I will always have some critiques. As mentioned before, the lyrics are very simple and feel slightly lazy at a point. All throughout the song, some lyrics/one-liners feel pretty lacking in substance. This results in some listens of the track where maybe you focus on the lyrical content a bit more, feeling a bit shallow. In fairness, this isn’t a pattern in Saam’s music; he has a lot of songs where he addresses real struggles in his life, or love, like in Ydoifeel or Locked in Love. So I chalk this more up to Saam wanting this to be more of a track focused on the “sound” and so changed his lyrical content accordingly, but maybe a little too far one way.

One more thing I’d like to discuss is: where do we see Saam taking this in, say, 3 years? While his tracks are consistently great and infectious, I wonder how much further he can evolve this sound. We are yet to even get an EP, let alone an album, from Saam, and I think that will be a very clear indication as to whether he’s as talented as he comes across from his singles. It’s much harder to make a full body of work people enjoy if it’s 8 songs of the same sound. I’m hoping he experiments a bit and tries something new while still retaining the key aspects of him that make his music so good. Variation, not reinvention.

## FINAL VERDICT

In all, this is, simply put, an incredible track. On par with “Locked in Love” (another track by Saam). He executes on his vision perfectly; he curates a sound for the listener that cannot be denied. For its purpose, it’s near perfect. Whether that purpose is for you is up to you, but it is for me. This is Saam’s magnum opus track, in my opinion, and it makes me so excited for his future releases… How can he top this? Where will he take this sound? For such a young talent, this is truly promising.

## RATING: 9.1/10
