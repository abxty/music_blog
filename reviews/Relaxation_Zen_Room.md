## Deathtoricky - relaxation zen room

It's time to revisit my aforementioned favourite artist of the last year, Deathtoricky. I'd like to take this review as an opportunity to both praise this EP, but also to delve more into Ricky as an artist overall. This 2025 EP was really the defining moment for Ricky, it propelled him into the mainstream of the underground (oxymoron, I know), and also established him as both an extremely promising talent, and a hitmaker. You may be wondering why I chose to review this 4 track EP first, over his more recent debut 12 track album '#ikfly', and the truth is, I think this EP is a perfect summary of what makes Ricky so great. So let's dive in.

## Motives

This EP is simply put, a collection of the most catchy and melodic music the UK has to offer right now. We start with 'Motives', Ricky's (deservedly so) most popular track. The beat to this track is produced by 'bxxxy', best known for being the genius behind the majority of Feng's production before his Weekend Rockstar era. The beat features a sample from Martin Solveig & Dragonette's track - 'Hello'. This sample, accompanied with these incredible synths, very reminiscent of a lot of the synths heard on 'What the Feng'. This combination of sample and synth are the crux of this song. What I love most about Ricky is his ability to understand production, he knows where his role sits in relation to the beat, he is not in competition to one up it, but to utilise it to its fullest. This track is the perfect party song, and it makes perfect sense as to why it blew up the way it did. (PS. Watch the music vid for this song, it's incredible)

## Breathing right

The next song is 'Breathing right'. This track is a stark contrast to 'Motives', with these dirty, punchy, layered synths, and a similarly distorted kick, 808 and snare. I'd also like to highlight this brief, yet impactful switch to a more clean, melodic synth from 00:15-00:23. It adds such a nice contrast from how bassy and dirty this song is, and makes that beat drop back in so much more impactful. Ricky really showcases his ability to flow and bounce on a beat, but also his incredible melodic talent. He tows this line between rapper/singer better than any other artist I know right now, and it's so refreshing. You can really hear Ricky's thick Irish accent come through on this track, and It's one of the things I think makes Ricky so unique. A lot of artists from the British Isles try to hide their respective accent, especially when trying to be more melodic. But Ricky uses it to his upmost advantage. It adds this charm and charisma to the track that simply cannot be captured by anyone other than an Irishman.

## Cologne

After this we have 'Cologne'. This song features a simpler, more stripped back production. We have this blues sample in the background, with (what I think to also be) a sampled guitar riff, and to round up the beat, a classic trap drum pattern. While simple, the drums and guitar riff bring so bouncy allow Ricky to weave his flow in and out of the beat and off beat effortlessly, and when you have a voice as good as Ricky's, that's all you need to create an infectious track. I don't have much more to say for this one: Simple, Catchy, Deathtoricky.

## daswhatiwant

Last and unfortunately least, we have 'daswhatiwant'. Unfortunately we have an anticlimactic ending to this otherwise great EP. This is certainly the most experimental track. My mine gripe with this track is the production on the vocals. There is some obvious pitching and alternative autotune on Ricky's vocals here, which really undermines what makes his performance so strong — that bright, melodic and clean sound. With this processing, his vocals come across as murky and sit much further back in the mix, and for a vocalist as talented as Ricky, that should never be the caseThe thing with Ricky is that his vocals are already so good on their own, and when you start to layer these effects onto them, you start to lose that charm and crisp sound he has. Alongside this, we have a pretty uninspired beat. With a simple piano chord progression and relatively stripped back intermittent synth,  I'm left wanting more. When I've just been served 3 incredibly layered, catchy tunes it's almost a shock when this track comes on. In fairness, the drum beat is, as always, catchy, however it is unfortunately not enough to save this track. 

## Conclusion

This EP is what I recommend to anyone wanting to get into Deathtoricky. It encapsulates his sound perfectly, and has some of his greatest highlights. What's best of all is that most of the criticisms I've mentioned about this EP, Ricky has addressed in his debut album. You can hear how much love Ricky has for the music, and there is so much promise in him. He could honestly take the full melodic route and be a massive success, but I truly hope he doesn't leave his Rap routes, as he is such a breathe of fresh air in this game.
